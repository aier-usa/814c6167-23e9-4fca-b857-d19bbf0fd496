How much money does Bill Gates have in his bank accounts? We wish we know the answer because it is a piece of news. Unfortunately, the answer is very difficult to get unless extreme and illegal hacking activities are employed. In order for us to understand two factor authentication. We need to know the concept of computer access control. Suppose that we have got a piece of information about Bill Gates' account: He stores his money at chase.com. We are lucky in one aspect. However, we still need two more pieces of information: username and password. computer access control is a fancy term used for a simple question: Are you Bill Gates? I don't know whether you are Bill Gates or someone else. I will let you in as long as you can provide evidence proving that you are who you said you are. Evidence falls into following categories: knowledge (something they know, typical examples are username, password and Personal Identification Number), possession (something they have, but others do not have), and physical characteristic( something they are, but others are not. For example, everyone's fingerprint is unique). Two factor authentication (also known as 2FA) is a method of confirming a user's claimed identity by utilizing a combination of two different pieces of evidence. Two-factor authentication is a type of multi-factor authentication. A good example for two factor authentication from everyday life is the withdrawing of money from a cash machine; only the correct combination of a bank card (something that the user possesses) and a PIN (personal identification number, something that the user knows) allows the transaction to be carried out.

Possessions and physical characteristic can add additional layers to security protection. Possessions include tokens that only authorized users can have. For example, a RSA SecurID token is something that is owned by a company and given to a particular computer user to plug into the computer. In order to use authentication based on physical characteristic, we need to have dedicated devices to read those physical characteristics.

Nowadays, mobile phone has been used as the authentication tool. The problems with mobile phone are includes:

  • Mobile phones can be stolen, potentially allowing the thief to gain access into the user's accounts
  • The mobile phone must be carried by the user, charged, and kept in range of a cellular network whenever authentication might be necessary.
  • If the phone is unable to display messages, such as if it becomes damaged or shuts down for an update or due to temperature extremes (e.g. winter exposure), access is often impossible without backup plans.
  • The user must share their personal mobile number with the provider, reducing personal privacy and potentially allowing spam.
  • Text messages to mobile phones using SMS are insecure and can be intercepted. The token can thus be stolen and used by third parties.
