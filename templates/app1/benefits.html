Easy to understand. Password hints are not passwords. They are used by computer users to facilitate the easy memorization of real passwords. Passwords are not randomly chosen. Instead, they are associated with "familiar concepts" that computer users can remember. Those "familiar concepts" becomes password hints. A password hint is valuable to the creator and it is useless to others. A password hint is similar to ciphertext (encrypted message)

No mathematical formula. Relax! Password management does not need to get mathematics involved.

No encryption key. Since we don't use encryption for our work, our users never need to worry about how to manage those keys. There is NO master key stored in our server. We don't need to take extra steps to protect our master keys.

Triple relationship. We store three pieces of information: website, username and password. In order to access sensitive information stored in a website, all three must be present. Instead of guarding password safely, you can free your password and guard website or username. You can use hints for all three pieces of information.

Partial Sharing. A user can have many password entries. Some of them can find more than 100 entries. Our system allows an owner to share a selected portion of the entries to trusted partners.
