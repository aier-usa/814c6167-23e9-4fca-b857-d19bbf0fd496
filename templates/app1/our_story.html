Our story

Max Li studied computer science in his college years in China. Back in late 1980's, there were not many computers on a college campus. As a result, the number of passwords to be remembered was limited. Password management was not yet an issue.

One keen observation about password management is that the number of passwords to be remembered never decreases. Over the years Max has accumulated a lot of passwords that he needs to manage. He first tried memorizing everything in his head. Then he tried writing down on random sheets of paper. When that proved inadequate, he wrote them in a notebook. He was still not happy.

Max tried several computer-based approaches. For example, he create an MS Word file in his own computer. But he later felt that an Excel spreadsheet was a better way to manage the problem. With the popularity of online file storage, he has stored files online on websites such as OneDrive and Google Drive. But he was still not satisfied.

When Max saw his wife used her notebook to write down everything, he thought to himself that he could do a better job of password management. He created a webapp called ihatepassword.com. The key innovation for the webapp is this: avoiding the encryption approach to password management. If he tried securing passwords using another (possibly more secure) password, the approach would ultimately fail as a hacker can crack any code. A hacker can never crack a well-designed password hint known only to the password creator - unless physical threats or forces are used!
